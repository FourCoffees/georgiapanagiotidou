**Sinem Görücü**  
_PhD Candidate_

Sinem Görücü is a postgraduate researcher in the Department of Informatics, King’s College London; researching the visualisation and communication of Machine Learning's environmental impact. She is also an interdisciplinary designer, educator, community organizer, and advocate working at the intersection of data, artificial intelligence, and design through the lens of social justice and intersectional feminism. She is the founder of Feminist Design, an organization that uses feminist frameworks to inform design processes and harnesses creativity as a tool for feminist advocacy.

**Sophia Worth**
_PhD Candidate_  

Sophia's research focuses on responsible data practices. She is a PhD candidate in Computer Science, part of the UKRI-funded Centre for Doctoral Training in Safe and Trusted AI in the Department of Informatics at KCL. Her PhD aims to evaluate the design and implementation of tools and methods for data ethics. Sophia has also recently undertaken research across numerous topics related to data and AI transparency, accountability for digital platforms in their accumulation and use of peoples' data, the data supply chain, and other related topics. Prior to her PhD Sophia worked across the research and policy teams at the Open Data Institute, where she continues to work as a Researcher alongside her PhD research. Sophia has previously completed an MSc in Human Geography at UCL where she studied environmental data governance through the lens of Science and Technology Studies, and an interdisciplinary BSc in Liberal Arts and Sciences where she graduated as a Sciences major. She is currently a member of the The King's Institute for Artificial Intelligence.
